The process of making this website

I began this project by trying to think of a fun way that kids could learn about the importance of vaccines. For a long time I had big problems finding ideas that I was satisfied with, so I started working on the home page. I wanted to have short and concrete information about the substainable development goals, COVID-19 and vaccines. I also wanted to implement this in a way where the users wouldn't feel overwhelmed by information either. My solution to this was to create cards, where (hopfully) the user would be drawn to hover over and read the information. Another reason that I liked that idea is that the user can choose which card to learn about. I collected the information on the cards from UN and WHO. This page is made primarily for children around the age 11-14.

Making the Corona-game

As an educated teacher I am very hesitant when it comes to making a game that envolves kids and social media. Even though an idea where kids get points for how much they recycle and share it on social media seems like a fine idea, there are a few conserns. In such a case it is likely that someone from a resourseful family can get more help at home than someone from a less resourceful family, and thus resulting in more points. This could again lead to a bigger gap in the social status of kids. This is the reasoning behind my careful approach to creating this game.

I came up with the idea while consulting with a teacher at a primary school. The goal of this game is to raise awareness about the unfairness of how vaccines are distributed around the world. By letting players experience the unfairness themselves, it might be easier to understand this problem.

Engaging society

As this website is meant to be educating children, it is important that the information is layed out in a way that encourages learning. It is also important that the information is relevant and filtered to fit the set age group. To ensure this I have consulted the information I have on this page with three different teachers. One working at a primaryschool, one advisor at a junior high school, and a teacher of social studies in the set age group.

Designing the webpage

I intended to design a webpage without too many distractions. The fonts that I use were chosen to make it easier for users read. To help me create a color scheme I used coolers.co. I find this to be a nice tool to help me create a color scheme, while still having great control over the colors I use. All the pictures (except the NTNU logos) are taken from pixaby.com and are free to use, with no attribution required. I also had to change the background colors of these pictures to fit the cards. The corona-game board is self-made, with images of the corona virus and vaccine from pixaby.
